History of Haworth labs

We are a US based pharmaceutical biotech research firm located in New Deutschland, Massachusetts. Incorporated in 1999 under the name PharmaGeist Partners, we invested in pure research into a specific subclass of underlooked MAOI molecules, none of which led to specific or clinically testable target therapies, but which nevertheless dramatically increased our understanding of poorly known neurochemical processes.

PharmaGeists’s fortunes shifted late in 2016, when we acquired a large batch of unexercised international pharmaceutical patents. Most of those drugs proved to be dead ends, but one drug looked like a promising candidate for Phase II trials. As part of our legal due diligence into the development background of that molecule (which dated back to post-war England) we found that even though it had not been in use in decades, its patent was in fact still active under the name “Joy,” and was held by a long-dormant research entity called “Haworth Labs.”

PharmaGeist assumed that meant that our excitement in Joy’s potential as a marketable US drug was unfounded. However, through an implausible-sounding and baroque series of notes (hand-written on large, television cue cards and delivered by a different mod-haircutted and dapper Vespa driver), legal representatives of Haworth Labs (England) established contact and transferred full ownership of the molecule to PharmaGeist on three conditions:

  1. That we retain the original branding and name of the molecule, Joy.
  2. That we rename PharmaGeist Partners as Haworth Labs (US).
  3. That we owe Haworth Labs (England) a trivial and probably-not-worth-mentioning favor at some point in the probably distant future.

And with that, PharmaGeist Partners became Haworth Labs (US) and is proud, in 2018, to offer an unprecedentedly powerful intervention to American sadness sufferers.
